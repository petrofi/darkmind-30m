## Conditional paths to deficits

- Turkish prose: Turk Kutuphaneciligi, article-level DergiPark and vetted Gutenberg works. Their combined conservative planning estimate is only 12.2M, so additional clearly licensed Turkish sources are required even if all three resolve.
- English general: GOV.UK and legacy OpenStax. Their combined conservative estimate is 18M; replacements or more official open-education/government collections are required.
- Technical: NIST, EUR-Lex, PMC, GitHub documentation and explicit-CC arXiv. Exact manifests and overlap controls must resolve before counting any capacity.
- Code/structured: GitHub allowlist and Stack Exchange could contribute, but both are high-PII/high-contamination and remain conditional. Additional low-PII official project archives are preferable.
- Controlled bilingual: DGT-Acquis does not verify Turkish. A new artifact with explicit Turkish-English coverage, reproducible licensing and at most a 10M contribution is required.

## Concentration and license mix

The approved acquisition caps pass: every exact dataset is below 15% of 200M, every family is below 20%, each Rust/MDN code ecosystem is at or below 5%, bilingual is 0%, Wikimedia is 0% and generated text is 0%. Approved licenses are CC BY-SA, Apache-2.0 OR MIT and PostgreSQL License.

Approved raw acquisition is projected at 0.27-1.22 GB from declared ranges. This is too little to meet category targets; the broader conditional pool could require hundreds of GB and must not be acquired until the source lock is upgraded.
